Chapter V — Philosophy

philosophy of life

नेति नेति
↳ tap to reveal

“Not this, not this” — or “neither this nor that.” When you discard every finite object using neti neti, you are not left with nothingness (nihilism). Instead, you arrive at pure, contentless awareness. It is the baseline consciousness that remains when all thoughts, sensations, and identities are completely removed.

अप्प दीपो भव
↳ tap to reveal

(Appo Deepo Bhava) “Be a light unto yourself.” Philosophically, it commands absolute self-reliance, urging you to look inward for truth rather than relying on external gurus, scriptures, or social dogmas. It serves as a dual metaphor: to act as your own lamp by using personal reason and direct experience to navigate the world, and to stand as an island by building an unshakable inner sanctuary that remains unaffected by external chaos.

One must imagine Sisyphus happy.
↳ tap to reveal

Absurdism is the philosophical idea that humans desperately search for meaning in a completely meaningless universe — and this clash is “the Absurd.” Instead of giving up, Camus says we should embrace the struggle. “One must imagine Sisyphus happy” means that when you accept that life has no built-in purpose, you become totally free. Like Sisyphus pushing his boulder forever, you find joy and power in the daily effort itself, not in the final goal.
